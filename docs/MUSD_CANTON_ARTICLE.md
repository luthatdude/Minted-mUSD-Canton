# mUSD on Canton Network: Bringing Institutional-Grade Stablecoins to DeFi

## The Problem No One Talks About

Stablecoins sit at the center of decentralized finance. They settle trades, collateralize loans, and provide the unit of account for an industry measured in hundreds of billions. Yet the infrastructure underlying most stablecoins was built for retail users on public blockchains with no native compliance framework, no privacy guarantees, and no mechanism for institutional oversight.

Institutions want in. They have the capital, the demand, and the regulatory mandate to participate. But they need something the current landscape does not offer: a stablecoin that meets their compliance requirements while still accessing the yield opportunities that make DeFi worth the complexity.

mUSD is built to solve exactly this problem.

## What Is mUSD?

mUSD is a stablecoin minted 1:1 against USDC reserves. Every mUSD in circulation is backed by a dollar sitting in a treasury — no algorithmic stabilization, no fractional reserves, no governance token propping up the peg.

What makes mUSD different is not the backing model. It is the architecture.

mUSD exists simultaneously on two networks: **Canton Network**, an institutional-grade distributed ledger built on DAML, and **Ethereum**, the dominant smart contract platform for DeFi. These are not two separate tokens. They are the same asset, moving between chains through a validator-attested bridge that preserves a single global supply at all times.

Canton handles what institutions care about most: compliance, privacy, and settlement finality. Ethereum handles what DeFi users care about most: yield generation, composability, and permissionless access.

The result is a stablecoin that is simultaneously institutional and decentralized — not as a compromise, but by design.

## Why Canton Network?

Canton Network is not another L1 competing for TVL. It is a purpose-built institutional ledger developed by Digital Asset, the company behind DAML (Digital Asset Modeling Language). Canton was designed from the ground up for regulated financial services, and it brings several properties that public blockchains cannot replicate:

**Sub-transaction privacy.** On Ethereum, every transfer is visible to every participant. On Canton, parties only see the portions of a transaction they are involved in. An institution minting mUSD does not expose its position to every other participant on the network.

**Multi-party agreements.** DAML templates encode legal agreements directly into the asset. Every mUSD on Canton carries an embedded reference to the Multi-Party Agreement (MPA) that governs its issuance and transfer. The contract is not a PDF sitting in a drawer — it is enforced at the ledger level.

**Byzantine fault tolerance.** Canton's consensus model provides finality guarantees that probabilistic blockchains cannot. When a Canton transaction settles, it is settled. There is no reorg risk, no MEV extraction, no block reorganization that might reverse a completed transfer.

**Native compliance hooks.** The Minted protocol on Canton includes a `ComplianceRegistry` that validates every mint, transfer, and redemption against a blacklist and freeze registry. This is not a wrapper bolted onto an ERC-20 — it is built into the DAML templates that define mUSD itself.

For institutions operating under KYC/AML requirements, these properties are not features. They are prerequisites.

## The Architecture: Two Chains, One Asset

The Minted protocol divides responsibility cleanly between its two execution environments.

### Canton: The Compliance and Settlement Layer

On Canton, mUSD is represented as a DAML template (`MintedMUSD`) with full support for splitting, merging, transferring, and burning. The template enforces dual-signatory security — both the provider and the owner must authorize any operation on the asset.

Key Canton components include:

- **CantonDirectMint** — Allows institutions to deposit stablecoins and mint mUSD directly on Canton. Rate-limited to prevent rapid supply changes, with automatic compliance checking on every operation.
- **CantonSMUSD** — A yield vault on Canton that mirrors Ethereum's staking vault. Yield generated by Ethereum strategies is synced back to Canton stakers through bridge attestations.
- **Leveraged Vaults** — Collateralized debt positions (CDPs) that support up to 10x leverage through atomic borrow-swap-deposit loops, all with health factor monitoring and liquidation protection.
- **ComplianceRegistry** — A set-based blacklist and freeze registry that gates every mUSD operation. Bulk import supports onboarding compliance lists at scale.

### Ethereum: The Yield and DeFi Layer

On Ethereum, mUSD is an ERC-20 token with role-based access control, supply cap enforcement, and blacklist capabilities. The Ethereum deployment is where backing reserves are put to work.

Key Ethereum components include:

- **DirectMintV2** — A 1:1 USDC-to-mUSD minting contract with configurable fees, 24-hour rate limiting, and supply cap enforcement. Users deposit USDC, receive mUSD; burn mUSD, receive USDC.
- **SMUSD (Staked mUSD)** — An ERC-4626 yield vault where mUSD holders can stake to earn yield. The vault follows the share-price model: as the Treasury generates returns, each share of smUSD becomes worth more mUSD over time.
- **TreasuryV2** — The protocol's reserve pool. All USDC backing flows here and is deployed into yield strategies including Pendle PT markets and Morpho lending vaults. Yield is distributed to smUSD holders.
- **Lending System** — A full collateralized borrowing system with `CollateralVault`, `BorrowModule`, `LiquidationEngine`, and Chainlink-powered `PriceOracle`. Users deposit collateral, borrow mUSD, and face liquidation if their health factor drops below threshold.

### The Bridge: Canton to Ethereum and Back

Moving mUSD between Canton and Ethereum is handled by the BLE Bridge — a 3-of-5 validator multi-signature system with rate limiting and replay protection.

The flow works like this:

1. A user on Canton calls `bridgeToEthereum()`, which burns their Canton mUSD and creates an attestation request.
2. Three of five independent validators verify the burn and sign the attestation using ECDSA (with AWS KMS integration for production key management).
3. The relay service watches Canton for finalized attestations and submits them to the `BLEBridgeV9` contract on Ethereum.
4. The Ethereum bridge verifies the validator signatures, checks the nonce for replay protection, and updates the supply cap — rate-limited to a configurable daily maximum.
5. The user can now mint equivalent mUSD on Ethereum.

The reverse flow mirrors this process: burn on Ethereum, attest, mint on Canton.

A critical invariant is maintained throughout: **Global Supply = Canton mUSD + Ethereum mUSD**. The bridge does not create or destroy value. It relocates it.

The 24-hour rate limiting on supply cap changes acts as a circuit breaker. Even if a validator set were compromised, the attacker could only increase supply by a bounded amount per day, giving operators time to detect and respond.

## Yield: Where the Returns Come From

All USDC reserves backing mUSD flow to the Ethereum Treasury. There is no idle capital sitting in a Canton vault. This is a deliberate design choice — Canton provides the compliance and settlement guarantees, while Ethereum provides access to DeFi yield.

The Treasury deploys capital through a strategy system managed by the `PendleMarketSelector`:

- **Pendle PT Markets** — Fixed-yield positions in principal token markets. The selector automatically evaluates available markets based on TVL, maturity, and expected APY, and handles rollovers when positions mature.
- **Morpho Lending Vaults** — Optimized lending positions that match supply with the highest-rate borrowers.

Returns flow back to smUSD holders. Because smUSD is an ERC-4626 vault, the yield accrual is automatic — the share price increases as the Treasury records returns, so holders earn yield simply by holding smUSD. No claiming, no compounding, no manual intervention.

For Canton users who stake through `CantonSMUSD`, yield is synced from Ethereum through bridge attestations. The same returns, the same economics — just settled on the institutional ledger instead of the public chain.

## Security: Defense in Depth

A cross-chain stablecoin protocol presents an expanded attack surface compared to a single-chain token. The Minted protocol addresses this through layered security controls:

**Rate limiting at every boundary.** NGINX rate-limits API access (10 requests/second read, 2 requests/second write). The bridge rate-limits daily supply cap increases. DirectMint rate-limits daily net mint volume. Each layer operates independently, so an attacker must breach all of them to cause meaningful damage.

**Multi-signature validation.** Bridge attestations require 3-of-5 validator signatures. Validators run independently with separate key management (AWS KMS in production). No single validator compromise can authorize a fraudulent attestation.

**Replay protection.** Every attestation carries a monotonically increasing nonce. The bridge contract tracks processed nonces and rejects duplicates. Attestation contracts are archived after use on Canton.

**Emergency controls.** Every critical contract supports pausing. The bridge, minting, and treasury contracts can be halted independently by authorized operators, enabling targeted incident response without shutting down the entire protocol.

**Formal threat modeling.** The protocol maintains a STRIDE-based threat model covering all components from the DAML ledger to the Kubernetes deployment. 98 security findings have been identified and resolved across multiple audit passes.

## Who Is This For?

mUSD is designed for participants who need both institutional compliance and DeFi yield:

- **Asset managers** who want stablecoin yield without sacrificing regulatory compliance. Mint mUSD on Canton, stake through CantonSMUSD, and earn Treasury-generated returns — all within a privacy-preserving, compliant settlement environment.
- **DeFi protocols** that need a stablecoin with transparent backing and institutional credibility. mUSD on Ethereum is a standard ERC-20 that integrates with existing DeFi infrastructure.
- **Treasury operations** seeking capital-efficient stablecoin management. The lending system supports leveraged positions for participants who want amplified exposure, with full liquidation protection.
- **Cross-chain participants** who operate across both institutional and public networks. The bridge enables seamless movement of value between Canton's regulated environment and Ethereum's open ecosystem.

## The Bigger Picture

The gap between traditional finance and decentralized finance is not a technology problem. It is an architecture problem. Institutions cannot participate in systems that lack compliance guarantees, and DeFi cannot reach its potential without institutional capital.

mUSD does not try to force institutions onto public blockchains or drag DeFi into walled gardens. It builds a bridge — literally and architecturally — between the two worlds. Canton provides the settlement and compliance layer that institutions require. Ethereum provides the yield and composability that makes the economics work.

The global supply is always conserved. The backing is always 1:1. The compliance is always enforced. And the yield is always accessible to both sides of the bridge.

That is what an institutional-grade stablecoin looks like.
